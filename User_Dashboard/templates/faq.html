Frequently Asked Questions

General Questions

Financial literacy is the ability to understand and effectively use various financial skills, including personal financial management, budgeting, and investing. It is crucial for effective money management and financial stability.

Being financially literate is important because it equips you with knowledge and tools to manage money effectively, make informed financial decisions, and increase your ability to achieve financial stability and goals.

Investing

Start by understanding your financial goals and risk tolerance. Learn about different investment options such as stocks, bonds, and mutual funds. Consider starting with low-risk investments or mutual funds if you are a beginner.

Budgeting

A budget is a financial plan for a defined period, often one month. It helps manage your finances by balancing expenses against income, ensuring you can cover necessary spending and save for future goals.

Effective ways to save money include setting clear savings goals, cutting unnecessary expenses, using automated tools to set aside savings, and regularly reviewing and adjusting your budget to improve savings.

Credit and Debt

Improve your credit score by consistently paying bills on time, keeping balances low on credit cards, avoiding unnecessary inquiries, and regularly checking your credit report to correct any inaccuracies.

Effectively manage debt by prioritizing high-interest debts, making more than the minimum payments, and considering strategies like debt consolidation or speaking with a debt counselor for structured repayment plans.
